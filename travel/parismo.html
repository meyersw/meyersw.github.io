Welcome to Paris Missouri

Places to go

Mark Twain's Birthplace

Mark Twain's Birthplace: this state historic site is the two room cabin where the famous author was born. You can also find artificats from Mark Twain's life such as a handwritten manuscript of "The Adventures of Tom Sawyer."

Located in nearby Florida, MO.

Mark Twain National Forest

Mark Twain National Forest:enjoy 1.5 million acres of beautiful public land. With over seven hundred and fifty miles of trails for hiking, horseback riding and mountain biking you are sure to find the adventure you seek.

Enjoy camping, fishing and river rafting Missouri's only National Forest.

Union Covered Bridge State Historic Site

Union Covered Bridge State Historic Site: built in 1871 Union Covered Bridge is only one of the four remaining covered bridges that represents the Burr-arch truss design.

The state historic site covers 1.24 acres and contains 0.2 miles of roadway and is a popular location for weddings.
